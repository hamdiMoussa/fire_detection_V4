How Forest Fires Contribute to Rising CO2 Emissions

Forest fires can release significant amounts of carbon dioxide (CO2) into the atmosphere. When trees and other vegetation burn, they release stored carbon into the air, adding to the amount of CO2 already present.
The huge desaster

The amount of CO2 released during a forest fire depends on the severity and duration of the fire, as well as the type and amount of vegetation burned. Large-scale forest fires can release millions of tonnes of CO2 into the atmosphere, contributing to global warming and climate change.
Not only co2 ...

In addition to CO2, forest fires can also release other greenhouse gases like methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O) into the atmosphere. These gases can trap heat and contribute to global warming.

When forests burn, vast amounts of the stored carbon is emitted,
scientists estimate that wildfires emitted about 8 billion tons of CO2 per year for the past 20 years. In 2017, total global CO2 emissions reached 32.5 billion tons, according to the International Energy Agency.

Smart For Green

Smart for Green is a startup created in 2022 within the local innovation center of Ras Jebel-Bizerte, its main activity was a design office that offers a multitude of services related to intelligent agriculture aimed at preventing fires and ensuring protection against forest fires. The Smart for Green startup is led by a talented and determined team of professionals who are passionate about their mission. Each one of them brings a unique expertise and an overwhelming passion to the company, and I am delighted to be working together to achieve a common vision

Company objects

Smart farming is a concept that aims to optimize agricultural practices by using information and communication technologies (ICT) to collect, analyze and use data on land, crops and weather conditions. The goal is to improve productivity, quality of agricultural products, sustainability and profitability of agriculture while preserving the environment, and Smart for Green works within this spectrum. The project is an initiative to preserve the environment and forest wealth. Relying on an R&D (Research and Development) expertise, the Smart4Green team proposes an intelligent solution that can intervene in the different phases of a fire while allowing prediction, prevention, detection and assistance in intervention, extension and evacuation in extreme cases. For the moment, Smart for Green plans to launch its project in the forest of Ras Jebel and in the forest of Rimmel, because these two regions are more threatened in Bizerte by fires and have experienced many ecological problems since the beginning of this decade.

Company sollution

The placement of the sensors in the target areas allows the collection of precise and instantaneous data on the state of the forest, measuring soil moisture and temperature. Once these data are collected and with the analysis of meteorological data during a heat wave, the system will be able to predict if there is a potential danger in specific areas, at this stage, the system allows to have a dashboard for real-time supervision and to alert the responders. In case of fire outbreak, the FFFP solution also allows detection with thermal cameras and notifies the forest rangers and firefighters of the region (Civil Protection) with a provisioning of information on the state of the fire with measurements of the temperature in the center of the fire, the rate of CO2 (carbon dioxide) which allows safe interventions of firefighters knowing the direction of the wind and the speed of propagation.
