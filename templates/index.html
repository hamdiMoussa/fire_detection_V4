CO2 emissions and climate change

Planet conditions...

Carbon dioxide emissions are the primary driver of global climate change. It’s widely recognized that to avoid the worst impacts of climate change, the world needs to urgently reduce emissions. Since humans are adding more CO2 to the atmosphere, that helps explain why temperatures are increasing around the world. Global warming describes the current rise in the average temperature of Earth's air and oceans, which is often described as the most recent example of climate change.

Forest and climate change

Forests act as carbon sinks...

Forests capture carbon from the atmosphere in the trees and soils. Some of this carbon ends up on the ground as fallen litter, and accumulates in the soil. Soils are one of the biggest carbon reservoirs on earth, storing more of this element than the atmosphere and the aboveground biomass put together. Deforestation and forest degradation account for about 10 percent of global greenhouse gas emissions caused by humans. Protecting and restoring forests can help mitigate climate change by absorbing carbon dioxide and providing other ecosystem services.

How Forest Fires Contribute to Rising CO2 Emissions

Forest fires can release significant amounts of carbon dioxide (CO2) into the atmosphere. When trees and other vegetation burn, they release stored carbon into the air, adding to the amount of CO2 already present.

The huge desaster

The amount of CO2 released during a forest fire depends on the severity and duration of the fire, as well as the type and amount of vegetation burned. Large-scale forest fires can release millions of tonnes of CO2 into the atmosphere, contributing to global warming and climate change.

Not only co2 ...

In addition to CO2, forest fires can also release other greenhouse gases like methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O) into the atmosphere. These gases can trap heat and contribute to global warming.
